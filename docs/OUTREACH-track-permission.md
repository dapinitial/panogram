# Outreach: asking a recorder to share their track

The clean path to publishing someone else's GPX on Panogram is their explicit yes.
Ask for **the original file sent directly** (email/DM) — cleanest provenance, and the
platform they posted it on stays entirely out of the loop.

The product honors a yes with: the `credit`/`credit_url` fields on the track (shown on
the overlay tooltip), `recorded_at` recency displayed, and removal on request, any time.

## Template (adapt, keep it human)

> Hi <name> — I found your <peak/route> trip report from <date> and it was genuinely
> useful (<one specific thing: the moat note, the descent line, the early-season
> timing>).
>
> I'm building Panogram (<url>) — 360° panoramas of places like <peak>, where a
> recorded GPX draws as a line on the actual mountain in the panorama. I'd love to
> feature your <date> track as the line on our <peak> capture, credited to you by name
> with a link wherever you like (your site, your profile). Happy to take it down any
> time you ask.
>
> If you're up for it, just email me the original GPX. And if not, no worries at all —
> thanks for the report either way.

## Rules (restating the standing decision)
- A yes covers **that person's own recording** — not the platform's database.
- No yes → no publish. Personal route research with their public report is fine;
  republishing isn't.
- Log the consent (email/DM) somewhere durable before the track goes live.
- `credit` empty = uploader's own track; `credit` set = someone else's, with permission.
